+ New Pokedex Print Help

How To Play

Make a Pokemon

  • Type any idea in the box. Sillier is better: "a butt pokemon named Gyatt", "a stinky sock pokemon", "a taco with a cape".
  • Smash GENERATE! and wait for the Pokeball to stop spinning (about 20 seconds - the AI is drawing).
  • You get a real card: art, HP, moves, and a secret backstory underneath.

Change it

  • Redraw: type what to change ("give it a crown", "make it angrier") and hit Redraw. Same creature, new look.
  • EVOLVE!: turns it into its next form - bigger, stronger, sillier. The name levels up too (Tacoloak became Tacolossus).
  • Type an idea BEFORE pressing EVOLVE! to steer the evolution (like: make it a dragon).
  • Tap any text on the card to edit it - name, HP, moves, even the flavor text. You're the game designer.
  • Want a new name? Tap the name on the card itself and type - the Redraw box only changes the picture.
  • Click the picture to see it BIG. Click anywhere (or press Escape) to close it.

Your Pokedex

  • Every Pokemon you make is saved forever in the Pokedex. Click any tile to visit it again.
  • Stage tabs at the top of a card let you flip between its evolutions.
  • Released a Pokemon by mistake? They're not gone - Dad can rescue them from the archive folder.

Print real cards

  • Go to Print, untick any cards you don't want, hit PRINT!
  • Cards come out exactly the size of real trading cards (63 x 88 mm). Cut inside the yellow border.

Choosing your artist

  • gemini - the super artist. Fast, and remembers what your creature looks like when you redraw or evolve.
  • bridge - the free artist. It draws through Dad's browser, so it's a bit slower and needs Dad's laptop set up. If it says "driver offline", ask Dad.
  • mock - practice mode. Instant blank picture, costs nothing. Good for testing names and moves.

If something goes wrong

  • "The Pokemon escaped! Try again!" - not your fault. The AI hiccuped. Just press the button again.
  • Page won't load? Make sure Dad's laptop is awake and you're on home Wi-Fi.

Pro tip: the weirder your idea, the better the Pokemon. The AI loves weird.
